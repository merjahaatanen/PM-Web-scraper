### **How to use this checklist**

This checklist is designed to help you quickly diagnose and resolve common issues on the Rainbow Stacking System. Follow the steps in order for each problem. If a step tells you to call Maintenance, or if you encounter any issues not covered, stop and report the problem immediately.

**SAFETY FIRST!**
*   **ALWAYS** use Lock-Out / Tag-Out procedures before performing any work inside the machine or reaching into moving parts.
*   **STOP** the machine and ensure all movement has ceased before reaching inside.
*   **NEVER** bypass safety devices or interlocks.
*   If you are unsure about any step, **STOP** and call Maintenance.

## 1. Visible Damage / Misalignment (seen in 12 work orders)
**Symptoms / what the operator sees:**
*   Bent or broken holder arms, brackets, or other structural components.
*   Safety light barriers or fences are bent, knocked out of alignment, or damaged.
*   Controller column is bent.
*   Loose or missing parts like blocks, bolts, or carriage guides.
*   Stacks of sheets sitting unevenly or "on another stack" due to misalignment.
**Likely cause(s):** Machine impacts, crashes, material handler collisions, general wear and tear, or loose hardware.
**Operator troubleshooting steps:**
1.  - [ ] **Visually inspect:** Carefully walk around the machine and look for any visibly bent, broken, loose, or missing parts, especially after a known impact or crash.
2.  - [ ] **Check safety light barriers:** If a light barrier is simply out of alignment (not broken), try to gently push it back into its correct position.
3.  - [ ] **Check fence sections:** If a safety fence section is open or slightly bent, try to gently push it back into place, ensuring any links or latches engage.
**When to stop and call Maintenance:**
*   If you find any bent or broken structural components (e.g., holder arms, large brackets, main beams).
*   If safety light barriers or fences are significantly damaged, cracked, or cannot be easily realigned.
*   If you find loose hardware that you cannot safely access or tighten.
*   If the machine continues to operate with visible damage, as this can lead to further, more severe problems.
**Related WO #s:** 2321, 3424, 7931, 7950, 9253, 10219, 10891, 12493, 12657, 13929, 15319, 18930
**Parts often needed (if any):** Arm holder (30-246-76581), Block (25-520-236691), Button holder (25-310-29260), Bolt (38-313-44891), Bracket holder (30-246-76581 / MFG 2-406-76-5811), Fixing device (25-206-06060), Guide carriage (25-406-04598).

## 2. Machine Not Referencing, Not Turning On, or X-Axis Issues (seen in 9 work orders)
**Symptoms / what the operator sees:**
*   Machine fails to reference or gets stuck during referencing.
*   Machine makes jerking movements or strange sounds when trying to reference.
*   Error messages related to X-axis, drive unit trip, or "cross rail raised".
*   Machine won't turn on at all.
*   Machine stops mid-reference.
**Likely cause(s):** Power interruptions, minor obstructions, dirty sensors, misaligned components, or internal electrical/mechanical issues.
**Operator troubleshooting steps:**
1.  - [ ] **Check for obstructions:** Carefully look along the X-axis rail for any debris, tools, or anything that might be physically blocking the crane's movement.
2.  - [ ] **Power Cycle (Reboot):**
    *   Turn the main power knob to the OFF position.
    *   Wait at least 5 minutes.
    *   Turn the main power knob back ON.
    *   Try referencing the machine again.
3.  - [ ] **Check Breakers:** If the machine won't turn on at all, or if you suspect a power issue, check the main breaker for the Rainbow machine. If it's tripped, reset it. (Only if safe and accessible, and you know which breaker it is).
4.  - [ ] **Clean Reflectors:** Locate the laser reflectors on the machine's rails (often at the ends). Gently wipe them clean with a soft, dry cloth to remove any dust or black smears.
**When to stop and call Maintenance:**
*   If the machine still won't reference or turn on after these steps.
*   If you hear loud grinding, snapping, or abnormal noises.
*   If the machine jerks violently or doesn't move smoothly.
*   If you see any visible damage to the rails, drives, or wiring.
*   If the X-axis error persists or you get specific fault codes you don't understand.
**Related WO #s:** 8045, 8068, 9349, 9668, 10159, 14421, 14794, 19844, 14153 (WO 2412 involved extensive repairs, requiring specialized technician involvement beyond operator scope.)

## 3. Incorrect Sheet Handling / Suction Problems (seen in 6 work orders)
**Symptoms / what the operator sees:**
*   TLF not picking up sheets correctly.
*   Suction cups are only partially on the sheet or in the air.
*   Sheets fall off the machine or slip, causing stacks to be messy.
*   Machine gets "stuck on vacuum" with or without a sheet.
*   Machine will not drop off a sheet or crashes into back stops.
**Likely cause(s):** Misaligned suction cups, faulty suction sensors, dirty sensors, issues with width measurement, or a previous sheet crash.
**Operator troubleshooting steps:**
1.  - [ ] **Recalibrate the machine:** If sheets are not being picked up or dropped off correctly, try running the machine's calibration sequence as per your standard operating procedure.
2.  - [ ] **Check for obstructions/debris:** Ensure the suction cups are clean and free of any debris or plastic sheeting that might prevent a proper seal.
3.  - [ ] **Clear stuck sheets/vacuum:** If the machine is "stuck on vacuum" or has dropped a sheet, manually remove any sheets causing the issue and reset the machine.
**When to stop and call Maintenance:**
*   If calibration does not resolve the issue.
*   If the machine consistently fails to pick up or drop off sheets correctly.
*   If the machine repeatedly gets stuck on vacuum.
*   If you suspect a sensor is faulty or there's internal damage from a crash.
**Related WO #s:** 2109, 5922, 7676, 13239, 13818, 14959
**Parts often needed (if any):** Sensor (IE5338), (Specific encoder/width sensor part number not provided in these WOs).

## 4. Belt Monitoring Error / Suction Pipe/Bracket Issues (seen in 5 work orders)
**Symptoms / what the operator sees:**
*   Error message "Belt monitoring error" displayed.
*   Machine stops unexpectedly.
*   Belt appears loose.
*   Suction is not coming on when picking up a sheet.
**Likely cause(s):** Bent brackets or beams that hold suction pipes/cups, loose cables, or physical damage from wear/tear or impacts.
**Operator troubleshooting steps:**
1.  - [ ] **Check for loose cables:** Inspect the cables in the area where suction cups are located, ensuring they are securely connected.
2.  - [ ] **Visual inspection of suction brackets:** Look for any visibly bent or displaced brackets/beams that hold the suction pipes or cups, especially in the middle section.
**When to stop and call Maintenance:**
*   If the "Belt monitoring error" persists after checking for loose cables.
*   If you find any bent, cracked, or severely misaligned brackets or beams supporting the suction system.
*   If there is no suction when picking up sheets, even after checking for obvious debris.
**Related WO #s:** 6874, 7321, 12513, 12798, 14963
**Parts often needed (if any):** Middle fixing device (25-206-66120), Center beams (25-406-66110).

## 5. Measurement Errors / Width Stop Errors (seen in 3 work orders)
**Symptoms / what the operator sees:**
*   Machine gives errors related to length or width measurement.
*   "Width stop error" message.
*   Sheets not being picked up correctly due to incorrect width measurement (suction cups off).
**Likely cause(s):** Dirty sensors, faulty measurement sensors/encoders, or pneumatic issues (e.g., leaking cylinders).
**Operator troubleshooting steps:**
1.  - [ ] **Clean measurement areas:** Gently clean any visible sensors or rods associated with length and width measurement with a soft, dry cloth.
2.  - [ ] **Recalibrate (if applicable):** If the issue affects sheet pickup, try recalibrating the machine (as per 'Incorrect Sheet Handling').
**When to stop and call Maintenance:**
*   If cleaning or recalibration does not resolve the measurement errors.
*   If errors persist or "width stop error" messages continue.
*   If you notice any air leaks (hissing sounds) around pneumatic cylinders.
**Related WO #s:** 4527, 2109, 12876
**Parts often needed (if any):** Cylinder (25-035-12892), Sensor/Encoder (generic).

## 6. Miscellaneous Errors (seen in 3 work orders)
**Symptoms / what the operator sees:**
*   "Empty slot X" or "slot not in position" error message.
*   Machine displaying "Axis not in position width" error.
*   Audible air leaks (hissing sounds) from the machine.
**Likely cause(s):** Sensor not resetting, detached sensor cables, or leaking air components.
**Operator troubleshooting steps:**
1.  - [ ] **Check for air leaks:** Listen for any hissing sounds indicating an air leak.
2.  - [ ] **Verify slot status:** If an "empty slot" error appears, ensure the designated slot is indeed empty or cleared.
3.  - [ ] **Check for detached cables:** Visually inspect areas around sensors for any cables that might have come loose or detached, especially if an "Axis not in position" error occurs.
**When to stop and call Maintenance:**
*   If you detect any air leaks.
*   If "empty slot" errors persist even after verifying the slot is clear (e.g., if a sensor is stuck).
*   If you find a detached sensor cable and are unsure how to safely reattach it, or if reattaching it does not resolve the issue.
**Related WO #s:** 3037, 6489, 16138
**Parts often needed (if any):** Air regulator (generic), (specific sensor/cable parts not provided in these WOs).
