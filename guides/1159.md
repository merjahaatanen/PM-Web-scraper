# Operator Troubleshooting Checklist: Insert 1 Screwdriver, Auto Reverse, Lever

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Symptoms | Troubleshooting Steps | When to Call Maintenance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Driver Mechanical Failure | - Driver not working<br>- Driver stopped going forward<br>- Stuck on reverse<br>- Driver not responsive | - Check if the driver is physically jammed<br>- Ensure the reverse lever is not stuck | - Internal gears are broken<br>- Reverse trigger rod is broken<br>- Internal return spring is broken |
| Air Supply Issues | - No air pressure<br>- Driver not working | - Check that air lines are securely connected at the T-junction | - Air pressure is present at 120 PSI but driver still doesn't work<br>- Air line leaks cannot be resolved by tightening |
| Alignment & Setup | - Inserts going in at an angle | - Check if the tool holder on the arm is centered and balanced | - Tool holder cannot be realigned by operator |
| Tooling Wear | - Drivers are "no good"<br>- Need replacement drivers | - Identify if the driver head is worn or damaged | - Replacement drivers are needed or current stock has incorrect heads |
