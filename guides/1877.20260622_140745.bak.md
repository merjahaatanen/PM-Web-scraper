# Operator Troubleshooting Checklist: TLF Intellistore (Rainbow Stacking System)- TLF211

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Symptoms | Troubleshooting Steps | When to Call Maintenance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Machine Not referencing / Movement / X-Axis Issues** | - Machine not referencing<br>- Crane jerks or makes sounds like it's stuck during referencing<br>- Machine stops and tries to reference again<br>- X-axis deactivating or not in sync<br>- Machine not turning on<br>- "Axis not in position width" error<br>- "Cross rail raised" error<br>- Drive unit errors (e.g., "Frequency Converter Trip triggered") | - Check for any visible obstructions or parts rubbing against each other along the machine's path.<br>- Perform a full power cycle: Turn off the machine's main power switch, wait 5 minutes, then turn power back on.<br>- If accessible and safe, reset the machine's breaker(s).<br>- Carefully wipe down the laser reflectors and sensors with a clean, dry cloth.<br>- Visually inspect for any loose or disconnected sensor cables that are safely accessible. | - If the machine still won't reference after performing all steps.<br>- If the machine continues to jerk, get stuck, or stops repeatedly.<br>- If X-axis errors persist or one side of the machine is visibly out of sync.<br>- If you suspect physical damage like bent beams or drive tire movement.<br>- If the machine won't turn on after resetting breakers. |
| **Sheet Picking / Suction / Measurement Issues** | - Machine not picking up sheets correctly<br>- Suction cups are only partially on the sheet or in the air<br>- Sheets fall off during transport<br>- Machine is under vacuum with a sheet underneath (sheets fell off)<br>- Machine has issues measuring for length or width, giving an error<br>- Sheets are stuck or not dropping off properly<br>- "Width stop error"<br>- Suction is not coming on when picking up a sheet | - Perform a full machine calibration.<br>- If calibration fails, try a full power cycle (turn off, wait 5 min, restart) then recalibrate.<br>- If safe to do so, manually open and close the width measurement sensor/encoder several times (if accessible).<br>- Check for any sheets stuck on the suction cups or any fallen sheets obstructing the path.<br>- Visually inspect for any obvious bent brackets, loose hardware, or obstructions around the suction cups and sensors. | - If calibration repeatedly fails.<br>- If suction issues persist after checking for obstructions and calibration.<br>- If error messages related to width, length, or picking continue.<br>- If you observe bent brackets, leaking cylinders, or other physical damage. |
| **Belt Monitoring / Bent Beams / Brackets** | - "Belt monitoring error" displayed<br>- Belt appears loose (check display error first)<br>- Suction pipes/cups appear misaligned or out of position<br>- Machine stops unexpectedly (especially after a crash)<br>- Machine is stuck on vacuum after a crash | - Visually inspect the suction pipe beams and associated brackets (especially in the center) for any obvious bends, cracks, or misalignment.<br>- Check for any loose cables around the suction area. | - If "Belt monitoring error" persists after visual inspection.<br>- If any beams or brackets appear visibly bent, cracked, or severely misaligned.<br>- If suction issues are clearly linked to physical damage to these components. |
| **Bent / Broken Holder Arms** | - A holder arm is visibly bent or broken (e.g., near the left-hand corner suction cups)<br>- Suction cup bracket is bent or showing cracks | - Perform a thorough visual inspection of all holder arms and suction cup brackets for any signs of damage, bending, or cracks. | - Immediately report any observed bent, cracked, or broken holder arms or brackets. |
| **General Mechanical Damage / Loose Components** | - Visible broken blocks, bolts, or parts on the machine<br>- Cable tray standoff bolt is broken<br>- Guide carriage is damaged<br>- Safety fence section is bent, open, or knocked down<br>- Loose hardware is observed | - Conduct a detailed visual walk-around inspection of the entire machine for any broken parts, loose bolts, bent components, or misaligned/damaged safety fencing or rails. | - Immediately report any broken, bent, or loose mechanical components, including safety fences or rails. |
| **Other Sensor / Light Barrier Issues** | - Error message: "Empty slot X or not in position"<br>- Error message: "Cross rail raised and placed switch still activated"<br>- Security light barrier is visibly out of alignment | - If you see an "Empty slot X or not in position" error, and the sensor is safely accessible from underneath the pallet, carefully use a hook with a handle to reset the sensor.<br>- Visually check if any safety light barriers or posts appear misaligned or knocked out of position. | - If the "Empty slot X" error persists after attempting to reset the sensor.<br>- If other sensor-related errors appear and cannot be cleared by simple resets.<br>- If safety light barriers are misaligned or damaged. |
| **Air System Leaks** | - Hissing sound coming from the machine<br>- Visible air leak from an air regulator or other pneumatic components | - Listen carefully for any hissing sounds indicating an air leak.<br>- Visually inspect air lines, fittings, and components (especially the air regulator) for signs of a leak. | - Immediately report any suspected air leaks, even minor ones. |
| **Controller Column Damage** | - The machine's controller column is visibly bent or damaged | - Visually inspect the controller column for any bends, cracks, or other signs of damage. | - Immediately report a bent or damaged controller column. |
