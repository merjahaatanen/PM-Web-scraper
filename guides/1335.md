# Operator Troubleshooting Checklist: Edge Finisher Laminate

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Symptoms | Troubleshooting Steps | When to Call Maintenance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Chipping, Wavy Edges, or Uneven Cut** | - Product edges are chipped (top, bottom, or both)<br>- Product has a "lip" after trimming<br>- Edges appear wavy or inconsistent<br>- Trimmer blades are cutting too much or not enough<br>- Trimmers are "backing in and backing out" | - Check the direction of the material's grain. Try to trim with the grain. Hand route against the grain if chipping occurs.<br>- Measure the material thickness accurately with a caliper. Adjust the machine's beam height to match the exact material thickness.<br>- Inspect the plastic overhang on the material. It should be approximately 1/4 inch per side. Notify your lead if it's excessively long.<br>- Check the buffers for wear or if they are hitting the machine frame. Replace worn buffers if you are trained to do so.<br>- (If trained) Make minor adjustments to the trimmer blades to improve the cut.<br>- Note if the issue occurs with specific material types or batches, as some are more prone to chipping. | - If adjustments to beam height or blade don't resolve the issue<br>- If chipping persists or worsens<br>- If blades appear dull, damaged, or cannot be adjusted properly<br>- If trimmers are "jumping in and out" or "backing in and out"<br>- If replacing buffers doesn't fix the problem, or if you are unable to replace them |
| **Material Getting Stuck, Damaged, or Feed Track Issues** | - Laminate or plastic gets stuck on the outfeed plate<br>- Panels are hitting the bottom exit guide or outfeed table<br>- Material is dragging or scratching<br>- Feed track hesitates or moves unevenly<br>- Conveyor belt has ceased movement<br>- Parts are crashing into guides | - Measure the material thickness accurately with a caliper. Adjust the machine's beam height to match the exact material thickness.<br>- Inspect parts for warpage before feeding. Do not feed heavily warped parts into the machine.<br>- Check the plastic overhang on the material. Notify your lead if it's excessively long.<br>- Regularly lubricate the feed track (e.g., 3 times a day or more) with WD-40 or approved lubricant.<br>- Inspect the buffers for wear or if they are hitting the machine frame. Replace worn buffers if you are trained to do so. | - If material continues to get stuck or damaged after checking material and making adjustments<br>- If the feed track is consistently hesitating, moving unevenly, or appears damaged<br>- If parts are scratching despite careful handling and buffer checks<br>- If the conveyor belt has stopped moving or appears to be seized<br>- If the outfeed plate or guides appear misaligned or broken |
| **Machine Not Starting or No Power** | - Machine "on" but does not turn on<br>- "No power" to the Edge Finisher<br>- Machine will not start<br>- Power does not restore after an emergency stop (E-stop) or safety switch activation | - Check all E-stop buttons (including any rear trip wires) and ensure they are fully reset (pushed in, then twist/pull out to release).<br>- Check door safety switches for proper closure and visible alignment. Ensure doors are securely shut.<br>- If the machine stopped due to parts feeding too close, ensure adequate spacing between parts before attempting to restart.<br>- Gently clean any visible dirt or dust around E-stop buttons if they feel stiff or are not resetting. | - If E-stop buttons are reset but power does not restore<br>- If safety switches appear damaged, broken, or cannot be easily aligned<br>- If the machine immediately trips again after restarting<br>- If the "feed track drive" is tripped and cannot be reset<br>- If controls (e.g., for beam height) are unresponsive after power is restored |
| **General Mechanical / Control Issues** | - Screws are stripped or loose<br>- Up/down switches for beam height are not working<br>- Parts are getting scratched (not due to chipping or return system)<br>- Orange belt on the return system is broken<br>- Feed track oiler is not working<br>- Hold down pinch point only goes down, not up | - Report any stripped or loose screws immediately; do not attempt to force them.<br>- Check for any visible obstructions around the beam height switches if they are unresponsive.<br>- Visually inspect the feed track oiler lines for any visible clogs, disconnections, or leaks.<br>- (When replacing buffers) Exercise caution to avoid stripping screws. | - If beam height switches are unresponsive or stuck<br>- If an orange belt on the return system is broken<br>- If the feed track oiler is not dispensing oil<br>- If screws become stripped or cannot be easily replaced<br>- If parts are being scratched due to mechanical component issues (e.g., missing roller on a limit switch) |
| **Dust Collection Issues** | - Excessive dust accumulation on or around the machine<br>- Main duct hose appears ripped or torn<br>- Trimmer is leaving a lot of debris on components<br>- Suction seems blocked or reduced | - Regularly vacuum visible dust from the controller, around the machine, and on top of components.<br>- Visually inspect all accessible main dust hoses for rips, tears, or disconnections. | - If a dust hose is ripped or torn<br>- If suction is noticeably blocked or weak, even after basic cleaning<br>- If excessive debris continues to accumulate despite regular cleaning |
