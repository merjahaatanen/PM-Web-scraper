# Operator Troubleshooting Checklist: Forklift # T4

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Symptoms | Troubleshooting Steps | When to Call Maintenance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Forklift Stays On / Electrical Contactors | - Forklift stays on after turning off the ignition.<br>- Electric contactor feels stuck.<br>- Forklift sparks when trying to connect the battery/charger.<br>- Horn gets excessive overuse or has an intermittent problem sounding off. | - If safe to do so, disconnect the battery cable.<br>- Tag the lift out of service immediately. | - Immediately for any of these symptoms, as they indicate a serious electrical fault. |
| Battery & Charging Problems | - Forklift has low battery.<br>- Battery is not keeping its charge, dropping within one hour of use.<br>- Battery is not holding charge and is smelling.<br>- Charging station shuts down on its own after connecting forklift. | - Ensure the battery is properly connected to the charger.<br>- Check the charging station display for any error messages.<br>- If battery is low or not holding charge, do not let it drop below flashing bars. | - If battery quickly loses charge or smells.<br>- If the charging station shuts down or doesn't charge the battery fully.<br>- If the battery consistently shows low charge despite proper charging. |
| Hydraulic Leaks / Hoses | - Lift blew a hose.<br>- Forklift is leaking excessively; found a puddle.<br>- Fluid leaking from a hydraulic hose, unit caught fire.<br>- Oil leak from the right hand side shock. | - Immediately stop using the forklift.<br>- Safely move the forklift to a designated maintenance area if possible, avoiding further leaks.<br>- Do NOT operate if oil is actively leaking or if there's any sign of fire or smoke. | - Immediately for any hydraulic fluid leak.<br>- Immediately if a hose has blown or if there's any sign of smoke or fire. |
| Lights or Horn Not Working | - Warning light is not working.<br>- Left side brake light is not turning ON.<br>- Forklift horn does not work properly (have to aggressively tap it hard). | - Check if the light bulb is visibly broken or loose.<br>- For the horn, try pressing it firmly in different spots to see if it responds. | - If a light remains non-functional after checking/replacing the bulb.<br>- If the horn does not work even after tapping. |
| Operator Seat Loose / Moving | - Seat does not stay in place, moving while driving. | - Visually inspect the seat for any loose hardware or broken latches.<br>- If you find easily accessible loose screws, carefully tighten them. | - If the seat continues to move after checking/tightening.<br>- If a latch mechanism or seat track is visibly broken. |
| Brake System Issues | - Brake master cylinder needs replacement (maintenance diagnosis).<br>- Brake alarm sounding off even with the brake off. | - If brakes feel spongy or ineffective, stop use immediately.<br>- Report any unusual sounds or alarms related to the brakes. | - Immediately if braking performance is affected.<br>- If the brake alarm sounds incorrectly or persistently. |
| Damaged Physical Components (Tires, Backrest) | - Bent backrest sub-assembly.<br>- Rear steer tires with excessive splits on the ends of the wheel. | - Visually inspect the backrest for bends or damage.<br>- Visually inspect tires for splits, cracks, or excessive wear. | - Immediately if the backrest is bent or damaged.<br>- If tires show excessive wear, splits, or damage. |
| Lift Drifting Down | - Lift is slowly dropping down with weight on it, without touching the controls. | - Do not operate the forklift with loads near maximum capacity if the lift is drifting.<br>- Observe the speed of drifting; if it increases, stop use immediately. | - Immediately if the lift starts drifting, especially with a load. |
| Hydraulic Oil Smells Smoky / Service Due | - Hydraulic oil smells smoky. | - Note any unusual smells, especially a smoky odor from the hydraulic oil system. | - Immediately report any smoky smell from hydraulic oil. |
