# Operator Troubleshooting Checklist: 3/4 " Edge Finisher, Solid, Technolegno/ Universal 280

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Symptoms | Troubleshooting Steps | When to Call Maintenance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bevel Cut/Finish Quality Issues | - Parts are coming back with a deep line, uneven finish, or chopped edges<br>- Material is chipping on the top or trailing edge<br>- The machine is making unusual loud cutting or grinding noises from the cutter area<br>- Bevel cutter motor feels excessively hot<br>- Main cutter sounds like it is struggling to cut | - Inspect parts for consistent bevel, lines, or chips after passing through<br>- Visually inspect the cutter area for any obvious damage or obstructions<br>- Verify that the top roller pressure adjustment is correctly set for the material thickness you are running<br>- Check if the top feeler wheel is moving freely and making proper contact with the part | - Cutter blades appear chipped, dull, or broken<br>- Machine is making loud cutting or grinding noises from the cutter area<br>- Bevel is consistently uneven, choppy, or leaving deep lines/scratches<br>- Parts are consistently chipping despite checking adjustments<br>- Cutter motor feels excessively hot or is not working<br>- Top feeler wheel is stuck or not spinning freely<br>- Adjustments to roller pressure or feeler wheel do not resolve the issue |
| Orange Belt Issues | - Orange belt is broken or ripped<br>- Orange belt has come off its track | - If the orange belt has come off its track, carefully and safely re-track it | - The orange belt is broken or ripped<br>- The orange belt repeatedly comes off track after re-tracking |
| Support/Guide Rollers & Feed Issues | - Parts or stiles are getting stuck inside the machine<br>- Roller wheels appear damaged, broken, or are leaning to the side<br>- Infeed guide feels worn out or has a noticeable lip<br>- A sensor is popping up and hitting parts | - Visually inspect guide rails and rollers for any debris or damage<br>- Clean guides and rollers if debris is present and accessible | - Roller wheels are damaged, broken, or leaning<br>- Parts are consistently getting stuck despite cleaning guides<br>- You notice a sensor popping up and hitting parts<br>- Infeed guides feel worn or have a 'lip'<br>- Top hold-down rollers appear misaligned or are not holding parts correctly |
| Camera/Monitor/Sensor Issues | - Camera view is incorrect or not showing both bevels<br>- Monitor is blank or not showing the product as it passes<br>- Go and no-go lights are not working<br>- Camera system is not working | - Check if the camera lens is clean and unobstructed<br>- Perform a machine control system restart (if your standard operating procedure allows for this) | - Camera view is consistently incorrect or not showing product properly<br>- Go/no-go lights are not functioning<br>- Monitor is blank or not displaying correctly<br>- Camera cables appear damaged or cut |
| Dust Collection/Hose Issues | - Dust collection hose or ducting is ripped, cracked, or has a hole<br>- Dust particles are coming out and accumulating on top of the machine<br>- Dust collector bag is ripped off | - Visually inspect all dust collection hoses, ducting, and the collection bag for any rips, holes, or cracks<br>- If you find a small tear in a hose, you may temporarily use black tape around the hole (as maintenance has done previously) | - Hoses, ducts, or the dust collector bag are ripped, cracked, or have large holes<br>- Dust is visibly escaping the collection system during operation |
| Buffer/Adjustment Mechanism Issues | - The machine is not automatically adjusting the buffer<br>- You are manually adding buffer multiple times<br>- The manual buffer adjustment knob feels stuck or is not moving forward | - If the manual buffer adjustment knob feels stuck, try gently pushing it back and forth | - Machine is not automatically adjusting the buffer<br>- Manual buffer adjustment knob is stuck or not responding correctly to adjustments |
| Miscellaneous Structural/Mechanical Issues | - A screw is missing from the pop-up table<br>- A guiding slat on the table is loose | - Visually inspect the pop-up table and guiding slats for any loose or missing hardware | - Missing screws or loose guiding slats that affect machine operation or safety |
| Machine Feed Track/Beam Height Hesitation | - Machine feed track hesitates when passing parts through<br>- You constantly need to adjust the beam height for smooth operation<br>- Operators must raise or lower beam height frequently (20+ times a day) depending on material thickness | - Observe the feed track for inconsistent movement or hesitation<br>- If adjusting the beam height temporarily resolves the hesitation, note this for maintenance | - Feed track hesitates or jams when parts pass through<br>- You need to constantly adjust beam height for smooth operation |
