# Operator Troubleshooting Checklist: Saw, Horizontal, Holzma

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Symptoms | Troubleshooting Steps | When to Call Maintenance |
| :---- | :------- | :-------------------- | :----------------------- |
| Sheet Feeding, GRIPPER & Clamping Issues | - Material not going in or hitting something<br>- Gap closer sticking up or bent<br>- Pusher arm leaning, bent, stuck, or not retracting<br>- Sheets going in crooked<br>- Machine crashing into sheets<br>- Micro infeed not level<br>- Micro infeed turning hard<br>- "Program fence moves not out of area of pressure beam" error<br>- Saw stops midway or past cut line<br>- Finger pusher pushing too many sheets or getting stuck<br>- Gripper arm appears damaged or loose<br>- Sheets are chipping or scratched by grippers/arms<br>- Air leak sound from grippers/hoses<br>- Cardboard stuck on clamps<br>- Saw guide not squared, causing non-squared cuts<br>- Saw taking 2 sheets at a time when set for 1<br>- Material vibrating during cut, causing chipping<br>- Material slipping from grippers causing undersized pieces<br>- Machine will not infeed sheet (potential magnetic strip failure) | - **Inspect Gap Closers:** Look for bent or lifted gap closers. Carefully try to push them back down if slightly bent.<br>- **Check for Obstructions:** Look for foreign material (plastic film, fall-off, cardboard) in the path of the sheets, micro infeed, or clamps. Remove carefully.<br>- **Examine Pusher/Gripper Arms:** Visually check if pusher arms or grippers are leaning, bent, or out of alignment. If slightly out, gently try to straighten them if safe to do so.<br>- **Check Micro Infeed Sensors:** Ensure sensors (especially the blue ones) are clean and free of dust or particles. Use an air nozzle to clean them.<br>- **Perform Air System Bleed:** If machine has an "air" system, turn off the main air supply, bleed the system, then turn air back on.<br>- **Manually Move Micro Arm:** If safe, gently try to move the micro arm back and forth and clean its track.<br>- **Adjust Hold Down Pressure:** If chipping/vibration occurs, try increasing the hold down pressure regulator slightly (e.g., from 5.5 bar to 6 bar) after consulting lead.<br>- **Power Cycle:** If an error related to infeed/retraction persists, try a full machine shutdown and restart.<br>- **Clean Grippers:** Use a scraper and lacquer thinner to remove plastic or wax buildup from under the grippers. | - Gap closer is severely bent or broken, requiring replacement.<br>- Pusher arm or frame is bent, loose, or twisted and cannot be easily straightened by hand.<br>- Micro infeed cylinder is stuck, broken, or requires internal repair/replacement.<br>- Stripped bolts or broken hardware on grippers/pushers.<br>- Persistent air leaks from grippers or hoses after checking connections.<br>- Saw guide is not squared and affects cut quality after basic checks.<br>- Errors persist after cleaning sensors and power cycling.<br>- Magnetic strip or cover is broken or detached from the metal strip. |
| Electrical, Air System & E-Stop Issues | - Machine has no power<br>- "E-stop triggered" error message<br>- Air leak sound from machine or air lines/nozzles<br>- Rear elevator table not coming down<br>- Label printer not working or printing labels<br>- Printer shows "ribbon out" error when ribbon is new<br>- On/off switch or main power switch is loose or not functioning<br>- Blade cover E-stop error<br>- Table keeps going over the limit<br>- Machine will not start up after a cycle | - **Check Power Switch:** Ensure the main power switch in the rear is connected and in the "ON" position.<br>- **Check All E-Stops:** Verify all E-stop buttons are disengaged (pulled out).<br>- **Check Blade Compartment Door:** Ensure the blade compartment door is fully closed and latched.<br>- **Full Machine Shutdown & Restart:** Perform a complete power cycle of the machine.<br>- **Inspect Air Lines:** Listen for hissing sounds. Visually inspect air hoses, fittings, and nozzles for damage, cuts, or disconnections.<br>- **Check Rear Elevator Chain & Block:** Ensure the chain with the emergency relief block is present and positioned correctly to engage the valve for lowering.<br>- **Printer Checks:**<br>    - Verify ribbon and media roll are loaded correctly and aligned.<br>    - Power cycle the printer (turn off for 5 minutes, then back on).<br>    - Ensure communication cables are securely connected.<br>- **Clean Sensors:** Clean any visible sensors on the table and undercarriage if table limit errors occur.<br>- **Check Side Sensors:** Inspect left side sensors for plastic or debris covering the sensor. | - Machine remains without power after checking main switch.<br>- E-stop error persists after checking all buttons and doors.<br>- Air leaks continue after checking accessible hoses and fittings.<br>- Rear elevator table not coming down due to mechanical issue with chain/block/valve.<br>- Printer issues persist after power cycling and checking media/ribbon/cables.<br>- Main power switch or E-stop button is physically broken or loose.<br>- Blade cover mechanism is damaged or continuously triggers E-stop. |
| Saw Computer & Software Errors | - "WIDTH STOP maintenance required" error<br>- Cadmatic software not fully loaded on startup<br>- System glitching, freezing, or throwing general error messages<br>- "ERROR COMPACTING DATABASE" or "NOT ENOUGH SPACE ON DISK" errors<br>- Saw slows down unexpectedly during cutting<br>- Trim cuts not cutting correctly<br>- Saw blade not returning home (when no mechanical fault is visible)<br>- Saw does not want to calibrate<br>- Machine not cutting or acting the same after a software update<br>- Cutting length is off (e.g., no 1/16" overage)<br>- Monitor turning off when done cutting<br>- Manufacturing maintenance past due | - **Full Machine Shutdown & Restart:** Perform a complete power cycle of the saw.<br>- **Allow Cadmatic to Load:** On startup, wait for the Cadmatic software to fully load and the machine to reference itself before interacting with the controls. Do not reset buttons during startup.<br>- **Clean Program Fence:** If calibration issues occur, clean the rear program fence thoroughly with an air wand to remove accumulated dust.<br>- **Monitor for Operator Error:** If the blade doesn't return home, review operating procedures to ensure proper sequence.<br>- **Check Monitor Settings:** If the monitor turns off, check monitor power saving (eco) mode settings; some may be adjusted to turn off after inactivity. | - Persistent error messages after power cycling and waiting for startup.<br>- Disk space errors that cannot be resolved by standard computer operations.<br>- Saw slowing down unexpectedly (may be a software setting changed by tech support).<br>- Trim cut settings or cutting length issues (parameters often require tech support adjustment).<br>- Calibration failure after thorough cleaning of the program fence.<br>- Any issues arising immediately after software updates.<br>- Manufacturer maintenance and audit required. |
| Physical Damage & Wear & Tear | - Lift table extension or components are bent or broken<br>- Plate support is broken<br>- Saw wipers making loud screeching noise<br>- Table top plates are broken, chipped, or cracked<br>- Igus energy chain bracket is broken<br>- Lift table height limit safety bar is bent<br>- Air table barring popped out or missing<br>- Table top missing slide bearings<br>- Air nozzles on table are broken<br>- Scratches on cut components, with a loose/stripped screw on sheets' path<br>- Blade deflection sensor plate broken | - **Visual Inspection:** Regularly inspect all machine components, especially gap closers, table tops, lift table extensions, and supports for signs of damage, bending, or wear.<br>- **Report Scratches:** If scratches are found, immediately inspect the area where sheets run for raised elements (e.g., loose screws, burrs) and report to Maintenance. | - Any component is visibly bent, broken, or has fallen off the machine (e.g., lift table extension, plate support, table top, Igus chain bracket, safety bar, air table barring/nozzles).<br>- Wipers screech persistently or have loose/stripped bolts.<br>- Table top has significant chips or cracks that affect material movement or cut quality.<br>- Loose or stripped screws are causing scratches on material, requiring specialized tools for removal/replacement.<br>- Blade deflection sensor plate is broken. |
| Scoring Blade Issues | - Scoring blade sounds like it's bogging down<br>- Scoring blade tool holder is hot or smells burnt<br>- Scoring blade not rotating easily by hand<br>- "Scoring blade malfunction" error message<br>- Saw stopped, display says "scoring saw cannot come down"<br>- "Setting for lifting of scoring not corrected" error<br>- Small belt snapped on saw<br>- Unusual noise from blade compartment, struggling to cut, metal shavings<br>- Scoring blade drive not functioning | - **Perform Blade Change Function:** If the blade is not moving or error messages appear, try initiating the blade change function on the control panel.<br>- **Check for Wedged Material:** Power off and lock out the machine. Carefully inspect around the scoring blade for any wedged material (phenolic, fall-off, plastic) between the blade and frame, or in the blade's path. Remove carefully.<br>- **Manually Move Scoring Blade (if safe/accessible):** If instructed by the control panel or if comfortable, try to gently move the scoring blade up and down to check for binding.<br>- **Check for Belt Damage:** Visually inspect the scoring blade belt (if accessible without disassembly) for rips or missing teeth.<br>- **Power Cycle:** A full machine shutdown and restart might clear some transient errors. | - Scoring blade belt is ripped or has missing teeth.<br>- Scoring blade remains stuck or will not move up/down after checking for obstructions and power cycling.<br>- Burn smell or excessive heat from the blade area.<br>- Persistent "scoring blade malfunction" or "setting for lifting of scoring not corrected" errors.<br>- Any metal shavings are consistently observed. |
| Main Blade & Cutting Quality Issues | - Main blade is not cutting or stops cutting<br>- "Main saw motor failure" error message<br>- Machine wouldn't work after a blade change<br>- Noise coming from the blade compartment, blade feels loose or is hot<br>- Cannot perform a blade change, door will not open or is locked<br>- Loose noises coming from the saw during operation<br>- Big blade stayed up and won't go down<br>- Operator dropped safety screw during blade change<br>- Saw main blade chipping parts when cutting<br>- Interrupted mid-cutting (thin strips of sheets) | - **Check Blade Compartment Door:** Ensure the blade compartment door is fully closed and latched.<br>- **Check for Wedged Material:** Power off and lock out the machine. Carefully inspect underneath the blade compartment and behind the blade deflection cover for any wedged material (phenolic, fall-off, plastic). Remove carefully.<br>- **Perform Blade Change Function:** If the door is locked for a blade change, try initiating the blade change function again.<br>- **Verify Blade Installation:** After a blade change, ensure the main blade is seated correctly and all hardware is finger-tight (Maintenance will do final tightening).<br>- **Power Cycle:** A full machine shutdown and restart might clear some transient errors. | - Main blade belt is ripped or broken.<br>- "Main saw motor failure" error persists after checking for obstructions.<br>- Blade is loose, hot, or making unusual noises after tightening.<br>- Blade compartment door remains locked or will not open for a blade change.<br>- Main blade arbor appears damaged or blade cannot be installed correctly.<br>- Big blade remains stuck up or won't go down.<br>- Essential hardware (like safety screws) are dropped or missing during blade change. |
| Dust Collection & Debris Clogs | - Machine leaving a lot of debris or dust on parts/floor<br>- Vacuum appears clogged or suction is weak (top or bottom)<br>- Excessive dust can be seen on the floor during/after cutting<br>- Saw still leaving dust on parts after operator cleaning<br>- Clogged lines preventing proper dust collection | - **Check Dust Collection Ports:** Inspect visible dust collection ports and hoses (e.g., on the hold-down beam, top section of the dust collection system) for blockages from plastic trimmings, fall-off, or other debris. Carefully remove any accessible clogs.<br>- **Clean Blade Compartment:** Thoroughly clean the blade compartment, especially around small slots leading to the dust collection system, as material can accumulate there.<br>- **Clear Underneath Saw:** Regularly clear out accumulated dust and debris from underneath the entire saw machine. | - Suction remains weak or ineffective after clearing all visible clogs and cleaning the blade compartment/underneath the saw.<br>- Blockages are deep within ducting or inaccessible areas, requiring disassembly.<br>- Persistent heavy dust indicates a systemic issue with the dust collection system that operator cleaning cannot resolve. |
| General Alignment & Measurement Issues | - One of the supporting legs is shifting<br>- Side aligner is not working<br>- Far left stop is too far forward, causing cuts to not be straight<br>- Saw is not cutting straight; pieces are coming out undersized from one end to the next<br>- Measurements seem off regarding clamps | - **Inspect Side Aligner:** Check the side aligner for any obstructions (e.g., plastic, fall-off) blocking its photo eye. Remove any debris.<br>- **Visual Check of Stops/Guides:** Lightly check if any stops or guides seem physically out of place. | - Supporting leg is shifting or machine is out of level.<br>- Side aligner metal bar is bent or stuck, or not working after checking for obstructions.<br>- Cuts are consistently not straight or pieces are undersized from one end to the next, indicating a machine calibration or guild adjustment is needed.<br>- Far left stop is visibly misaligned and affecting cut quality. |
