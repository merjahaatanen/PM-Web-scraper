# Operator Troubleshooting Checklist: Saw,Horizontal, Holz-Her

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Symptoms | Troubleshooting Steps | When to Call Maintenance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pneumatic & Air Leak Issues** | - Saw stops during a cut<br>- Displays "0013 Error pneumatics", "pneumatic error", or "Disturbance" on screen<br>- Audible air leak (hissing, whistling) coming from the machine<br>- Saw blade does not return to home position<br>- Saw blade does not raise or lower properly for changing<br>- Clamps are not working or are weak<br>- Machine will not start due to an air leak | - Listen carefully for any hissing sounds indicating an air leak.<br>- Visually inspect easily accessible air hoses and connections for obvious rips, gashes, or disconnections.<br>- Ensure air supply to the machine is on and at proper pressure. | - If an air leak is detected or suspected<br>- If any pneumatic error message persists<br>- If the saw blade does not return to home or won't raise/lower<br>- If clamps are not working correctly<br>- If the machine won't start due to an air leak |
| **Machine Stops & Obstructions** | - Saw stops abruptly during a cut<br>- Machine turns off unexpectedly<br>- Blade gets stuck during operation<br>- Safety switch triggers<br>- Material is pushed out by the arms and saw won't run<br>- Saw stops midway or after a certain distance<br>- Displays "width drag fault" error<br>- Saw carriage is stuck or not moving<br>- Machine shuts down with "lower power voltage" message<br>- Dust accumulating on machine and floor | - IMMEDIATELY check for any loose pieces of particle board, slivers, or debris stuck under the beam, in the blade's path, or in the saw carriage tracks. Carefully remove any obstructions.<br>- Check if material is dragging on the table or if grippers are catching on the material.<br>- If the saw stops after a blade change and pushes material out, try performing an "air cut" (run a program without material).<br>- If the machine displayed "lower power voltage" and has a lot of build-up, vacuum dust and debris around the carriage and blade compartment. | - If stuck material is difficult to remove or requires special tools<br>- If the "width drag fault" error persists after checking for material drag<br>- If the saw carriage remains stuck after clearing visible debris<br>- If the machine keeps stopping even after clearing obstructions and trying an "air cut"<br>- If the machine shuts down with a "lower power voltage" message<br>- If dust collection is failing or duct hose is clogged |
| **Air Table Issues (Missing Nozzles/Airflow)** | - Difficulty moving full size sheets on the air table<br>- Air table not creating enough lift<br>- Air buttons/nozzles/balls/bearings are missing from the table surface<br>- Air table nozzles are covered or taped over | - Visually inspect the air table for any missing or damaged air buttons, nozzles, or ball bearings.<br>- If any nozzles are taped over, remove the tape.<br>- If material is hard to move, check for large accumulations of dust or debris on the air table surface that might block airflow. | - If air buttons, nozzles, or ball bearings are missing or broken<br>- If the air table performance is poor even after clearing debris and uncovering nozzles<br>- If air flow seems weak or uneven |
| **Carriage, Pusher & General Mechanical Issues** | - Pushing arm failed down or is loose<br>- Small plates or table tops have fallen off or are loose<br>- Loud grinding or banging noise from the saw carriage<br>- Saw carriage is not slowing down properly when returning to home position<br>- Pushers broke or loose<br>- Clamp arm is twisted or bent<br>- Rubber sponge under the beam needs replacement<br>- Emergency Stop switch fell off<br>- Broken bolts found near motor bracket | - If the Emergency Stop switch has fallen off, carefully reattach it by twisting it back into place. | - If any pushing arm or clamp arm is damaged, loose, or not functioning<br>- If any small plates or table tops are loose or have fallen off<br>- Immediately report any grinding, banging, or unusual noises from the saw carriage<br>- If the saw carriage is not slowing down correctly<br>- If pushers are broken or loose<br>- For any bent or broken mechanical components<br>- If the rubber sponge under the beam needs replacement<br>- If bolts or hardware are found loose or broken near the motor |
| **Blade & Cutting Quality Issues** | - Saw not cutting straight<br>- Material is chipping on the bottom edge<br>- Saw blade is not turning on<br>- Saw blade makes a strange noise (e.g., grinding)<br>- Difficulty removing/changing the main or scoring blade (e.g., screw stuck or overtightened, blade not raising)<br>- Saw will not cut 1'' cuts in series mode<br>- Saw gripping material but stops when pressing button to cut (no error alarms) | - Change the main and scoring blades if they are dull or if you hear unusual noise during operation.<br>- When changing blades, ensure the retaining screw is tightened securely but DO NOT overtighten it.<br>- If the blade is not turning on, try performing a machine re-calibration from the control panel.<br>- If material is chipping, try running the cut slower.<br>- If the saw will not cut 1'' cuts in series mode, check for and clean any visible photo sensors with a clean cloth or ear swab.<br>- If saw grips material but fails to cut without alarms, clean the width sensor with an ear swab. | - If chipping persists after changing blades and slowing the cut speed<br>- If the blade retaining screw is stripped or stuck<br>- If the saw blade is not turning on after re-calibration<br>- If the blade makes a strange noise that doesn't go away after changing blades<br>- If the blade does not raise or lower properly for replacement (this can also be a pneumatic issue)<br>- If cutting quality issues persist after trying these steps |
| **Computer & Control System Issues** | - Saw computer screen stopped working or went blank<br>- Displays "width stop maint required" error message<br>- "Low disk space" alert for C drive<br>- Saw width stop is located too far back, making it hard to position material<br>- Motor stopped midway through cycle | - If "width stop maint required" message appears, click "OK" for all. If the machine does not calibrate automatically, go to "Tools" and select "Calibrate" on the screen.<br>- If the monitor appears frozen or blank, try resetting the monitor or power cycling the machine.<br>- If motor is stopped midway, attempt a monitor reset. | - Immediately for any computer screen issues, blank screens, or if the machine computer is unresponsive<br>- If "Low disk space" alerts appear<br>- If calibration issues persist or the width stop is incorrectly positioned after attempting operator calibration |
| **No Fault Found / Intermittent Issues** | - Machine stops after every cut, but no error message appears<br>- Operator reports an air leak, but no leak is found during technician inspection<br>- Machine keeps turning off with a "pneumatic error" but then works normally for technician<br>- Saw carriage is reported stuck, but works normally for technician | - Power cycle the machine completely (turn off, wait 30 seconds, turn back on).<br>- Carefully observe the machine's behavior to gather more specific details if the issue re-occurs (e.g., exact timing, sounds, specific movements, other messages). | - If the intermittent issue happens frequently or prevents production<br>- If the machine still shows reported symptoms after a power cycle |
